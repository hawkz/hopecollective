Hope Collective

Olympic Triathlon for
Dads Lane Community Association

Hello! I’m Steve from Hope Collective. We’re based in the Hazelwell area of Birmingham (around Kings Heath and Stirchley) and we’re all about finding a little bit of hope in community.

As part of this we are working alongside local residents and others to reinvigorate Dads Lane Community Association building on the corner of Pineapple Road and Cartland Road. The building has been a great community resource for over 50 years but in the last few years it has reduced in use and those faithfully serving the building have expressed a need for renovation and new vision.

I am currently training for the Birmingham Triathlon and hope to raise much-needed support for the work of the Association. I really believe that this building is an amazing asset and can play its part in making the community a great place to live, work and grow.

But wait... I’m not asking for your money here. The building will, of course, need funds to make it fit for purpose but what it needs more than anything is your contribution of time and skills. So I’m asking if you would consider donating the promise of a morning, an afternoon or an evening (or days if you’re able) to offer skills and energy to enable this building fulfil its potential.

I’ve never done a triathlon before (let alone an Olympic one!) and the training programme is pretty gruelling on my now-40-year-old body! The race is in Sutton Park on Sunday 16 June 2019 and involves a 1,500m open water swim followed by a 40k cycle ride and a 10k run.

I would love for you to sponsor me in this event for this brilliant cause.
